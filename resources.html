Resources and Tools



Books

This book provides an in-depth overview of fire ecology, including its ecological and evolutionary aspects.

"Fire Ecology" by Malcolm Gill

Explores the history of forest fire management and its ecological implications.

"Wildfire: A Century of Failed Forest Policy" by George Wuerthner

This book delves into the ecological role of mixed-severity fires in various ecosystems.

"The Ecological Importance of Mixed-Severity Fires: Nature's Phoenix" edited by Dominick A. DellaSala and Chad T. Hanson

Articles

• Canadian Forest Fire Behavior Prediction (FBP) System: Used for assessing fire behavior and danger in Canada, integrating factors like fuel, weather, and topography.
• Phoenix Fire Model: A model that simulates the spread of fire in forest landscapes, including factors like fuel types and weather conditions.

Fire Risk and Behavior Analysis

• Earth Observing System Data and Information System (EOSDIS): Offers satellite data and imagery for monitoring and analyzing fire events globally.
• Sentinel Hub Playground: Allows you to explore satellite imagery, including Sentinel-2 imagery that can be used for fire monitoring

Fire Mapping and Monitoring Platforms

• Fire Ecology Journal: Publishes research and articles related to fire ecology, fire behavior, and fire management.
• Fire Effects Information System (FEIS): Provides information on the effects of fire on various plant species and ecosystems.

Fire Ecology Research and Resources

• BehavePlus: A fire modeling system that predicts fire behavior, fire effects, and the spread of wildfires. It's widely used by fire managers and researchers.
• FLAME: Fire, Land, and Atmospheric Modeling of Natural Events is a modeling system for simulating wildland fires and their effects on the landscape.

Fire Behavior Simulators

• Fire Information for Resource Management System (FIRMS): Provides near real-time fire data and mapping using satellite imagery. It's useful for monitoring active fires worldwide.
• Geospatial Multi-Agency Coordination Group (GeoMAC): Offers interactive maps displaying wildfire locations and perimeters in the United States.
• Cal Fire Incident Map: Provides current information on wildfires and incidents in California.

Fire Mapping

• National Interagency Fire Center (NIFC): Offers tools for fire weather prediction, including the Wildland Fire Assessment System (WFAS) and Fire Weather Planning Forecast.
• Fire Weather Intelligence Portal (FWIP): Provides fire weather data, forecasts, and analysis tools.
